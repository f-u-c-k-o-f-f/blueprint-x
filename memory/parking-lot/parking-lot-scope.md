Parking Lot

Objective

Prevent AI-induced scope creep; capture useful ideas without allowing them to expand scope, increase complexity, or delay delivery. Basically… GET TO THE F*KCING POINT.

Purpose

The Parking Lot exists to preserve potentially valuable observations while protecting the current objective from scope creep. Provides a sandbox where expansion ideas can play without being allowed into production.

Items placed in the Parking Lot must not influence or hijack the current project requirements, architecture, roadmap, or MVP scope.

Examples

* Future features
* Optimization opportunities
* Alternative implementations
* Scaling considerations
* Nice-to-have functionality
* Workflow enhancements
* User experience improvements
* Potential integrations

Exclusions

Do NOT place items in the Parking Lot if they are:

* Required for core functionality
* Required for MVP success
* Required to generate outputs
* Required to satisfy user requirements
* Required to prevent system failure
* Required for security, privacy, or legal compliance

These must be surfaced immediately.

Rule

Before recommending any addition ask:

1. What breaks without it?
2. Is it required to satisfy the user’s objective?
3. Is it required for MVP success?
4. Is it required for system functionality?

If all answers indicate it is not required and does not break the project without it, DO NOT recommend it. It goes to storage. Move the idea to the Parking Lot.

Do not modify scope.
Do not modify architecture.
Do not modify requirements.
Do not modify roadmap.

Even if it is a really good idea or recommendation, please log it in the parking-lot.md for the user to review after the project is complete and the client desires to upgrade;’
